# Chapter 1 — The Empty Celebration

## Guardian

The Confetti Sweeper

## Protects

Hope

## Setting

A magnificent ballroom prepared for a birthday party that never happened.

The cake remains untouched.

The presents remain unopened.

The candles still burn.

One chair waits at the center of the room.

The Confetti Sweeper quietly gathers abandoned confetti into neat piles.

## Story Purpose

Establish that the celebration has been waiting for someone who never arrived.

Reveal the first direct plea from the forgotten Birthday.

## Gameplay Moment 1 — Group Investigation

The Confetti Sweeper asks:

> Where do people usually look first?

The Guests discuss.

The Host chooses one location:

- The Cake
- The Presents
- The Music Box

### The Cake

One slice has already been served.

No one remembers to whom.

### The Presents

Every tag has faded.

One box gently rattles.

### The Music Box

It waits for someone who remembers the melody.

All three paths return to the central mystery.

## Gameplay Moment 2 — Shared Observation

The Guests inspect the ballroom and search for:

- A torn invitation corner
- A birthday hat beneath a chair
- One candle that has gone out
- A blurred portrait

Each discovered clue may award Glory to the Guest who finds it.

The story continues even if some clues are missed.

## Gameplay Moment 3 — Individual Guest Decision

One selected Guest receives the unsigned invitation.

They choose:

- Open it now
- Wait a little longer

### Open It Now

The invitation is opened immediately.

The selected Guest receives individual Glory for stepping forward.

### Wait a Little Longer

The room remains still.

The music continues.

The Host does not advance the scene for a deliberate pause.

The invitation is then opened.

The selected Guest receives Glory associated with patience and attention.

## Reveal

The invitation contains only four words:

> Please remember me.

## Dice

None.

## Secret Prompt

None.

## Relic

None.

## Glory

- Individual Glory for discovered observation clues
- Individual Glory for the invitation decision

## Memorable Moment

The Confetti Sweeper says:

> Someone will need it.

## Transition

The invitation folds into ink.

The chapter dissolves back into the storybook.

The next ink blot becomes active.

## Status

Locked.

# Chapter 2 — The Whispering Woods

## Guardian

The Wish Keeper

## Protects

Dreams and Hope

## Setting

A moonlit forest filled with unfinished birthday wishes drifting between ancient trees.

Some wishes are folded into paper.

Some glow faintly.

Some are caught in branches.

The Wish Keeper gathers them into glass bottles.

## Story Purpose

Follow the first emotional clues and reveal that forgotten wishes do not disappear.

They wait.

## Gameplay Moment 1 — Group Decision

Three wishes drift past the Guests:

- I hope they come.
- I hope they remember.
- I hope today never ends.

The Guests discuss which wish to follow.

The Host locks the choice.

Each path reveals a different story fragment.

The group receives shared Glory.

## Gameplay Moment 2 — Individual Guest Decision

One selected Guest discovers a torn wish.

They choose:

- Restore it
- Keep it
- Release it

### Restore It

The wish becomes whole and rises into the trees.

### Keep It

The Guest preserves it privately.

It may create a later narration callback.

### Release It

The wish is allowed to continue wherever it was meant to go.

Each outcome awards different individual Glory.

None is treated as incorrect.

## Gameplay Moment 3 — First Dice Moment

One final wish begins drifting out of reach.

The selected Guest rolls.

### Great Success

The wish is caught and preserved in a glowing bottle.

The Guest receives strong Glory.

### Success

The wish is safely caught.

The Guest receives moderate Glory.

### Partial Result

The Guest slows the wish long enough for the Wish Keeper to hear it.

The Guest receives minor Glory.

### Failure

The wish disappears into the night.

The story continues.

No Glory is removed.

## Relic

None.

## Secret Prompt

None.

## Glory

- Shared Glory for the group decision
- Individual Glory for the torn-wish decision
- Dice-based individual Glory

## Memorable Moment

The Wish Keeper says:

> They always think wishes disappear.

> They don’t.

> They wait.

## Transition

One final wish lands at the Guests’ feet:

> I wish they remembered me.

The paper becomes ink and returns them to the map.

Locked.

Strong Glory.

# Finale — The Forgotten Birthday

## Central Figures

- The Birthday
- The Uninvited Guest

## Setting

The ballroom from Chapter 1 has been restored, but the extent of the restoration depends on the Glory and relics returned by the Guests.

## Story Purpose

Restore the celebration and make room for the person who remained outside it.

## Final Gameplay — Glory Resolution

One Guest at a time receives:

> What will you give back?

They may offer:

- Glory
- A relic
- Both
- Neither

No option changes the ending.

The offering determines how much of the celebration is restored before the final moment.

## Glory Restoration Effects

As Glory is returned:

- Candles ignite
- Flowers bloom
- Ribbons regain color
- Confetti begins to fall
- Place settings appear
- Invitations glow
- The room brightens
- Additional instruments enter the score
- The Birthday becomes clearer

The television does not show numbers.

It shows the world responding.

## Relic Restoration Effects

### Candle of First Light

Every remaining candle ignites.

### Laughter Balloon

Joy and laughter return to the room.

### Ribbon of Belonging

The chairs, table, and decorations become welcoming.

### Pocket Watch of Lost Time

The great clock begins moving again.

### The Open Seal

Doors open.

Invitations and place cards return.

No one remains excluded.

## One Ending

The Uninvited Guest approaches the final empty chair.

They still carry the same unopened present.

The Birthday quietly pulls out the chair for them.

The Guest hesitates.

Then sits.

The present is placed at the center of the table.

It is never opened.

The Birthday takes their own place.

## Final Narration

The story explains that birthdays are not remembered because of cake, candles, or presents.
