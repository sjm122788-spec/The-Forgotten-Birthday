# The Forgotten Birthday

Status: LOCKED CORE VISION

## Experience

The Forgotten Birthday is a cooperative, illustrated storybook experience for a group gathered around a television.

It is not an RPG, combat game, or traditional party game.

The television presents:

- The living storybook
- The illustrated map
- Memory Window transitions
- Storybook Plates
- Narration
- Guardian interactions
- Puzzles and shared moments
- The restoration finale

Each Guest uses a phone for:

- Private decisions
- Secret prompts
- Dice moments
- Glory
- Relics
- Individual interactions

The Host reads the narration aloud and advances the story.

## Guests

Players do not portray fictional characters.

They enter the story as themselves and are known simply as Guests.

There are:

- No character classes
- No avatars
- No combat abilities
- No character statistics
- No traditional levels

This keeps the ending personal when the Birthday speaks directly to them.

## Artistic Identity

The experience resembles an illustrated storybook that occasionally asks the people reading it to participate.

The illustration is always the visual hero.

Movement is primarily communicated through narration.

Animation is reserved for atmosphere and major emotional payoffs.

The world breathes through:

- Directional lighting
- Candle flicker
- Dust
- Smoke
- Mist
- Drifting wishes
- Ribbons
- Falling petals
- Glowing memory threads
- Subtle environmental motion

Characters remain mostly still, like figures in an illustrated tableau.

## Storybook Map

The map is a black-and-white illustrated spread divided into three Acts.

The route, ink blots, unlocks, and progression are React/SVG overlays rather than elements baked into the artwork.

The map begins mostly monochrome.

As the story progresses, restrained watercolor details may return to the completed regions.

## Memory Window Ritual

Each numbered chapter begins through the following ritual:

1. The next ink blot becomes active.
2. Ink stirs and unfurls from the blot.
3. A Memory Window forms in black and white.
4. Color gradually seeps into the glass.
5. The camera pushes through the window.
6. The Storybook Plate replaces it.
7. Ambient movement begins.
8. Narration appears.

The Memory Window is a fragment of the chapter environment, not a portrait of the Guardian and not a literal summary of the scene.

The hidden interlude, The Quiet After, breaks this visual language and does not use an ink blot or Memory Window.

## Guiding Principles

The Forgotten Birthday is not about winning a story.

It is about sharing one.

Every mechanic should help someone remember.

The television never celebrates the score.

It celebrates the world becoming whole again.
