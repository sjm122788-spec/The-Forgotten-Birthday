# Guardians of The Forgotten Birthday

Status: MOSTLY LOCKED

Guardians are not quest givers.

Each Guardian has quietly continued caring for something the world is forgetting.

Movement is usually described through narration.

Each Guardian should require no more than:

- One primary illustrated pose
- One optional emotional/story pose
- Subtle ambient movement around them

---

## The Confetti Sweeper

Chapter:
The Empty Celebration

Protects:
Hope

Description:
A tiny elderly mouse who patiently sweeps abandoned confetti into neat little piles.

They continue preparing the ballroom because they believe someone will eventually need it.

Defining line:
“Someone will need it.”

Primary pose:
Sweeping a small pile of confetti.

Story pose:
Holding or offering the unsigned invitation.

---

## The Wish Keeper

Chapter:
The Whispering Woods

Protects:
Dreams and Hope

Description:
An ageless caretaker who collects unfinished birthday wishes before the wind carries them away.

They store the wishes in small glowing bottles.

Primary pose:
Collecting or listening to drifting wishes.

Story pose:
Holding a bottle containing one glowing wish.

---

## The Candle Keeper

Chapter:
The Candle Keeper

Protects:
Light

Description:
A quiet caretaker who tends the last birthday candles still burning throughout the world.

Each flame represents a remembered birthday.

Primary pose:
Tending the endless candles.

Story pose:
Holding or shielding the Candle of First Light.

---

## The Celebration Baker

Chapter:
The Baker Without a Recipe

Protects:
Celebration

Description:
A warm, flour-covered baker who remembers every birthday they have baked for but no longer remembers the recipes.

They remember why the cake matters, even when they cannot remember how to begin.

Primary pose:
Standing or working beside the empty recipe book.

Story pose:
Presenting the completed cake.

---

## The Parade Marshal

Chapter:
The Parade That Never Marched

Protects:
Tradition

Description:
A theatrical, dignified parade leader carrying a silver baton.

They remember every turn and every song but have forgotten the first step.

Primary pose:
Standing still with baton lowered.

Story pose:
Baton raised as the parade begins.

The Giftmaker’s Workshop

Working with ribbon at the bench.

Story pose:
Holding or finishing one meaningful gift.

## The Bridge Keeper

Chapter:

## The Uninvited Guest

Recurring figure and hidden interlude focus.

Represents:
The fear of not being remembered, included, or valued as deeply as one hoped.

Description:
A timeless, ambiguous figure in a slightly oversized charcoal wool coat, scarf, ordinary shoes, and simple hat.

Their face is naturally obscured through posture and directional shadow.

They are not sinister, magical, or villainous.

They always carry the same carefully wrapped present with the same burgundy ribbon.

They stand outside the light until the finale.

They are present only when their appearance strengthens the composition.

They should not appear in every chapter.

Primary pose:
Standing quietly and holding the present.

Final pose:
Placing the present at the center of the table or approaching the offered chair.

Canon name:
The Uninvited Guest

Production shorthand:
The Waiting Guest

---

## The Birthday

Finale.

Description:
The Birthday is not a villain.

They are the person, memory, or spirit the world has forgotten.

They should not appear royal, grand, or triumphant.

They are gentle, quiet, and gradually restored through the Guests’ Glory.

The Birthday pulls out the final chair for the Uninvited Guest.

Final line:
“I knew you’d remember.”
